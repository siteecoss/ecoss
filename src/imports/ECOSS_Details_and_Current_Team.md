The details of the Economics Students Society (ECOSS) according to its 2075 Constitution are as follows:

\*\*Institutional Overview\*\*

\- \*\*Name\*\*: The organization is named \"Economics Students Society\" in English and \"अर्थशास्त्र विद्यार्थी समाज\" in Nepali.

\- \*\*Abbreviation\*\*: It is commonly referred to as ECOSS.

\- \*\*Office Address\*\*: The office is located at the Central Department of Economics (CEDECON), Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences, Tribhuvan University, Kirtipur-10, Kathmandu.

\- \*\*Legal Nature\*\*: It is an autonomous, non-profit, non-political, and non-governmental social organization.

\- \*\*Scope of Work\*\*: Its primary jurisdiction includes educational institutions under the TU Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences where economics is taught, though branches can be opened across Nepal if necessary.

\- \*\*Established\*\*: 1985 AD

\*\*Objectives and Policies\*\*

\- \*\*Educational Advancement\*\*: The society aims to improve the educational standards and professional knowledge of economics students.

\- \*\*Research and Coordination\*\*: It focuses on promoting research-based education and fostering coordination between professors, students, and educational institutions.

\- \*\*International Interaction\*\*: It seeks to increase student interaction with national and international stakeholders in social, economic, and political sectors.

\- \*\*Institutional Support\*\*: It provides necessary assistance to educational institutions and conducts related educational activities.

\*\*Membership\*\*

\- \*\*Types of Members\*\*: There are four categories: Ordinary, Founder, Life, and Honorary members.

\- \*\*Ordinary Membership\*\*: All students regularly studying economics at the TU Central Department are automatically ordinary members.

\- \*\*Founder Membership\*\*: Individuals in the initial committee at the time of registration are founder members.

\- \*\*Life Membership\*\*: Granted to individuals who provide special support and pay a one-time fee of NPR 2,000.

\- \*\*Honorary Membership\*\*: Awarded to distinguished social workers or prestigious citizens; however, they do not have voting rights.

\- \*\*Eligibility\*\*: Members must not have criminal records involving moral turpitude or be disqualified by Nepalese law.

\- \*\*Termination\*\*: Membership can be terminated for dishonesty, non-payment of fees for over two months, or holding a political appointment or position in a student union.

\*\*Organizational Structure\*\*

\- \*\*General Assembly\*\*: This is the supreme body of the society, consisting of all members except honorary members.

\- \*\*Executive Committee\*\*: A 15-member committee is formed to manage the society.

\- \*\*Officers\*\*: The committee includes a President, Vice President, Secretary, Joint Secretary, and Treasurer.

\- \*\*Advisory Board\*\*: Includes one Patron (Department Head), three Advisors, and two Co-advisors.

\- \*\*Semester-Based Allocation\*\*:

\- If three semesters are active: The first semester provides the Joint Secretary and 2 members; the middle semester provides the Vice President, Secretary, and 3 members; the upper semester provides the President, Treasurer, and 3 members.

\- If two semesters are active: The lower semester provides the Secretary, Vice President, and 3 members; the upper semester provides the President, Treasurer, Joint Secretary, and 3 members.

\- \*\*Gender Requirement\*\*: There must be at least one female representative from each semester in the committee.

\- \*\*Tenure\*\*: The committee serves for a one-year term.

\*\*Operations and Finance\*\*

\- \*\*Funding\*\*: Sources include membership fees, voluntary donations, grants from local or government bodies, and income from organized programs.

\- \*\*Account Management\*\*: The society's bank account is operated through the mandatory signature of the Treasurer along with either the President or the Secretary.

\- \*\*Quorum\*\*: 60% attendance is required for a General Assembly (51% for a re-called meeting). The Executive Committee requires 51% attendance for its meetings.

\- \*\*No Confidence Motion\*\*: A motion against any official requires a 2/3 majority of the total members to pass.

\- \*\*Constitutional Amendments\*\*: Requires a 2/3 majority of the General Assembly and subsequent approval from the local authority.

\- \*\*Dissolution\*\*: If the society is dissolved, its remaining assets become the property of the Government of Nepal
